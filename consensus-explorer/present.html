The problem

How does a network of strangers agree on the truth with no one in charge?

A consensus algorithm is the mechanism that lets a decentralised network validate transactions, prevent double-spending and stay in sync — without trusting any single party.

The framework

The Blockchain Trilemma

Every consensus design spends a fixed budget across three pillars. Optimise for two, and the third gives ground.

  • Scalability — throughput & block speed (TPS).
  • Security — resistance to 51% & Sybil attacks.
  • Decentralisation — how open node participation is.

    A closing distinction

    Public vs Enterprise

    The sharpest way to organise all nine: are participants anonymous, or known? That single fact decides which trade-offs a chain can afford.

    Open · anonymous

    Public, permissionless

    Anyone can join, so the network must be trustless. Sybil resistance is bought with a scarce real resource — energy, capital, storage, or destroyed value — and finality is usually probabilistic or economically enforced.

    PoWPoSDPoSPoHPoCPoB
    Closed · identified

    Enterprise, permissioned

    Participants are legally known, so trustlessness is unnecessary. These chains use highly efficient voting or hardware mechanisms with instant finality — at the cost of open decentralisation.
